SDG 14 aims to conserve and sustainably use oceans, seas, and marine resources for global development. It emphasizes reducing marine pollution, particularly from plastics and land-based activities, and protecting marine ecosystems to ensure they remain healthy and productive. SDG 14 also addresses challenges like ocean acidification, overfishing, and destructive fishing practices by promoting sustainable management and science-based approaches. Additionally, it calls for the conservation of at least 10% of coastal and marine areas and the elimination of harmful fisheries subsidies. This goal highlights the essential role oceans play in supporting biodiversity, livelihoods, and climate regulation.
